Creating an educational board game for children

As teaching methods evolve, games are increasingly finding their way into the classroom. This prompted a board game publisher to ask how the games it produces could be used in schools. The aim of the project was to provide the client company with a global research document on possible game applications, as well as a first prototype solution. For this project, the main challenge was to design an experience spanning two phases: the child at school and the child with his parents.

Duration

1 month

Role

UX Designer, Facilitator

Sector

Games publishing

Researching and understanding individuals

Since the school environment involves many different stakeholders, we have chosen to focus on the parent-child-teacher trio.

The research phase was divided into two main components. Firstly, documentation work was carried out on various subjects such as people's relationship with games, the parent-child relationship in education, the parent-school relationship and specialist publications on the subject.

In parallel, semi-directive interviews were conducted with teachers, parents and experts in education and child psychology. As a UX Designer, I took part in drawing up the interview guides and writing the research report.

Concevoir

Once the research phase had been completed and the team had finalized the literature review, we began researching and formulating our concept through a creativity session that I personally led.

At the end of this session, the team debriefed and chose to select the following idea: Create a game to teach children how to receive, give and return compliments. We then worked on refining our solution, and "Le Monde des Compliment" was born. It's a kit that allows each child to create a "compliment" card and give it to one of their classmates. Children slip the cards they receive into a special album. They can then show their parents the cards they've received.

It was also important to us that the game should be simple in its mechanics, friendly in the relationships it creates, and rewarding by ensuring that every child gets a compliment.

Producing and prototyping

In order to produce a first prototype of our solution, a moodboard with the different graphic tracks chosen was created to decide which one we would go for. I then took part in the production of the game's visuals, as well as the final assembly of the game prototype and its box.
